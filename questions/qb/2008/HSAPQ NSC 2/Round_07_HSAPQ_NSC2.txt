answer: {diffraction} [accept {Fresnel diffraction} before “{Fraunhofer}”]

The magnitude of one type of this phenomenon is governed by a namesake v-parameter given by the Cornu Spiral, and its Fraunhofer type occurs for Fresnel number less than one. Bragg's law explains how x-rays experience it when meeting crystals, and the Davisson-Germer experiment showed that electrons experience it. It can be explained by Huygens ' Principle, and constructive interference illustrated that it happens to light in Young's double-slit experiment, showing its wave nature. For 10 points, name this phenomenon in which a wave bends around an obstacle.
