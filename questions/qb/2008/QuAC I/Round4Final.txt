answer: Mount {Kilimanjaro}

This inactive volcano has three cones, the largest of which is Kibo, and its associated national park is located in Moshi. First summitted in 1889, four-fifths of its glacial ice caps have disappeared. The story that it was given as a birthday present by Queen Victoria to Wilhelm II has been debunked, and it is considered the largest free standing mountain in the world. For ten points, identify this mountain in Tanzania, the tallest in Africa.

answer: {alkenes} [or olefins]

Wilkinson's catalyst is used to hydrogenate these compounds, and the Ziegler-Natta catalyst is used to polymerize them. They are the starting material in the oxo process, and according to Zaitsev's rule, the more thermodynamically stable one will form in eliminations. They are named using E-Z nomenclature, and one equivalent of hydrogen gas is required to reduce these compounds to alkanes. Also known as olefins, these are, for 10 points, what unsaturated molecules that have at least one carbon-carbon double bond?

answer: {Marfan} syndrome

This disease shares many heritable conditions with Ehlers-Danlos syndrome. It is caused by the presence of an autosomal dominant allele on chromosome 15, and many patients with this disease die from leaks to major arteries, such as the aorta. Sufferers of it have a mutation in the gene that codes for fibrilin, which contributes to the durability and strength of connective tissue. Sufferers of it are usually very tall and have extremely long limbs. For 10 points, name this disease, which may or may not have afflicted Abraham Lincoln.
