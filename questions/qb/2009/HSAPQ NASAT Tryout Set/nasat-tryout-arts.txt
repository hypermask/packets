answer: "{Fanfare} for the {Common Man}"

This piece makes use of an "F, B flat, F" ascending motif. It was commissioned as part of a program honoring World War II servicemen by Eugene Goosens, the conductor of the Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra. It received its ultimate name when the composer heard a radio speech by Vice-President Henry Wallace. The composer re-used this melody in his Third Symphony, and dedicated this piece to the figure "who... was doing all the dirty work in the war and in the army." For 10 points, identify this composition by Aaron Copland, whose title indicates that it was written for the ordinary American.
